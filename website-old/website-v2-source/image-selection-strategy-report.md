# Nature.Illustrated Image Selection Strategy

Date: 2026-06-18

Scope: Visual strategy for selecting images from the private `Nature.illustrated.reference_images` repository for the Nature.Illustrated website collage and an SMBE conference poster.

No source image files or website code were modified for this report.

## Visual Direction

Nature.Illustrated should feel elegant, nature-inspired, scientific, artistic, Scandinavian/minimal, and credible for researchers. The strongest direction is to use restrained, premium, mostly white or cream-ground scientific illustrations for the website, and reserve higher-drama editorial work for the SMBE poster.

The current website palette uses warm paper/cream, forest green, sage, charcoal, and terracotta. Images that harmonize best with this palette have clean grounds, graphite or ink linework, muted blues and greens, controlled warm accents, and enough negative space to breathe.

## Suggested Website Collage Composition

Hero/anchor image:

- Use `Kopie_von_pangolin1.pdf` as the primary anchor, ideally as a large portrait tile on the left or center.
- Preserve the graphite texture and white space. Avoid cropping too tightly.

Supporting images:

- `Kopie_von_Bowhead.pdf`: use as a clean landscape or medium tile, ideally with the full body visible.
- `Kopie_von_Blastocyst_illustration.png`: use as a square or circular-feeling tile to introduce cellular biology.
- `Fern_A5.png`: use as a narrow portrait accent or small tile.
- `Kopie_von_CellCover_2D3D_mammoth.png`: use as a dramatic cropped detail, focusing on the tusk/head/cave-line transition.
- `MBE_Cover_BlackRhino.tiff.tiff`: use as an optional small landscape tile for conservation/editorial impact.

Recommended layout:

- Large portrait anchor: pangolin.
- Top/right medium landscape: bowhead whale.
- Mid/right square: blastocyst.
- Bottom/side narrow accent: fern.
- Bottom or background-adjacent larger crop: mammoth detail.
- Optional small landscape: rhino.

Cropping guidance:

- Pangolin: keep generous white space; avoid clipping the head or scale structure.
- Bowhead: keep full body if possible; avoid over-cropping the tail.
- Blastocyst: use as a clean square/circular tile; avoid pairing with `Kopie_von_Blastomere_illustration.png` prominently because they are visually repetitive.
- Mammoth: crop to the strongest conceptual area; avoid large dark empty regions.
- Rhino: use a panoramic crop and avoid making it dominate the calm homepage tone.
- Journal-cover images with mastheads or publisher logos should not be used prominently on the homepage unless cropped to remove those elements.

  - `Kopie_von_Blastocyst_illustration.png`
  - `MBE_Cover_BlackRhino.tiff.tiff`
  - `Kopie_von_Cover_current_biology_landscape.pdf` or `Kopie_von_Untitled_Artwork.jpg`

Poster message:

- The poster should immediately communicate: scientific illustration by an evolutionary biologist for researchers.
- It should show both artistry and practical research communication services: graphical abstracts, journal covers, conference visuals, posters, figures, lab logos, and outreach illustrations.

## Filename And Alt Text Recommendations

Use descriptive, lowercase filenames for exported web assets:

- `pangolin-graphite-scientific-illustration.webp`
- `bowhead-whale-scientific-illustration.webp`
- `blastocyst-cell-illustration.webp`
- `mammoth-evolution-scientific-illustration.webp`
- `fern-botanical-line-drawing.webp`
- `black-rhino-conservation-illustration.webp`

Alt text convention:

- Describe the subject, medium/style, and scientific context.
- Keep alt text concise.
- Avoid keyword stuffing.

Example alt text:

- "Graphite-style scientific illustration of a pangolin head and scales."
- "Scientific illustration of a bowhead whale on a white background."
- "Watercolor-style blastocyst illustration showing early embryonic cells."
- "Conceptual mammoth illustration combining cave art and realistic anatomy."
- "Minimal black line drawing of a fern frond."
- "Black rhinoceros conservation illustration at sunset."
